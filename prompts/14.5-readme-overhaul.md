## What PulseCraft is (use this framing; adapt as you write)

PulseCraft is an **AI-agent-driven change communication intelligence service** that turns raw marketplace change artifacts (release notes, Jira tickets, feature flags, incidents, docs) into **BU-ready, personalized notifications** — with safety gates, audit trails, and human-in-the-loop review for anything uncertain or regulated.

Three specialist LLM-backed agents collaborate at six judgment gates:
- **SignalScribe** — decides *worth communicating? ripe? clear enough?* (gates 1, 2, 3)
- **BUAtlas** — decides *is this BU affected? is the drafted message worth sending?* (gates 4, 5) — runs in parallel per BU
- **PushPilot** — decides *is now the right time to send?* (gate 6)

A deterministic Python orchestrator sequences them, enforces invariants (quiet hours, rate limits, approved channels, dedupe, PII redaction, MLR-sensitive content detection), and routes anything uncertain to human review. Every decision is audited and replayable. Delivery renders to Teams cards, email, push notifications, or portal digest via Jinja2 templates.

The distinctive design choice is the **agent-vs-code split**: agents express *preferences* (what they think should happen); deterministic policy code enforces *invariants* (what must or mustn't happen). When agent preference conflicts with policy, policy wins and both decisions are logged for calibration.

Current state: walking-skeleton complete. Eight representative fixtures exercise all gates end-to-end with real LLMs (Claude Sonnet 4.6) at ~$0.15 per change processed. Non-GxP scope for v1; synthetic data only.
