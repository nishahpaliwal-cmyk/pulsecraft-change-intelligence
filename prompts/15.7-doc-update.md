## Demo results

Two real runs from the demo UI, captured 2026-04-23. Both runs used live LLM agents (Claude Sonnet 4.6) against synthetic fixtures. The screenshots below show the full decision documents produced by the pipeline — every gate, every hook, every BU evaluation, and the terminal state.

### Scenario 03 — a change affecting multiple BUs

**Fixture:** `change_006_multi_bu_affected_vs_adjacent.json` — an analytics portal change adding rolling date windows, cross-report filter panel, named filter presets, JSON export format, and async large-export processing. Rollout date 2026-05-01, nine days from ingestion.

**SignalScribe reasoning** — all three gates decide cleanly that the change is worth communicating, is ripe for notification, and is clearly described enough to hand off:

![Scenario 03 — SignalScribe decides COMMUNICATE (0.90), RIPE (0.90), and READY (0.85) with reasoning grounded in the artifact. Impact areas identified: reporting dashboard filtering, data export workflow, enrollment summary report UI.](design/demo/screenshots/Screenshot_scenario_03-1.png)

Gate 1 confidence 0.90, Gate 2 confidence 0.90, Gate 3 confidence 0.85. The agent grounds every decision in specific artifact content: the date is 9 days from ingestion (within the 14-day "imminent rollout" threshold for RIPE), the before/after behavior is described at the field level (enabling READY), and the new capabilities are enumerated concretely.

**BUAtlas parallel fan-out** — three candidate BUs evaluated simultaneously, each with isolated context. This is the architectural centerpiece:

![Scenario 03 — three BU cards side-by-side showing Delta BU, Epsilon BU, and Zeta BU each with their own Gate 4 AFFECTED verdict and Gate 5 WORTH_SENDING verdict, with distinct BU-specific reasoning in every card.](design/demo/screenshots/Screenshot_scenario_03-2.png)

All three BUs returned AFFECTED on Gate 4 and WORTH_SENDING on Gate 5 — but with distinctly different reasoning. Delta BU cited its Clinical operations reporting dashboard consolidation initiative. Epsilon BU cited its ownership of the analytics_portal surface and the async export behavior change. Zeta BU cited ownership of both impact areas simultaneously. Each BU's message draft was personalized — push_short named different specific actions, why_relevant named different owner roles. Nothing was copy-pasted across BUs.

**HITL routing on priority** — the pipeline reaches pre_deliver, detects a priority_p0 trigger, and routes to human review:

![Scenario 03 — blue "Routed to human review" banner showing priority_p0 trigger for bu_zeta, followed by the operator review panel with Approve, Reject, Edit, and Answer question buttons.](design/demo/screenshots/Screenshot_scenario_03-3.png)

The operator review panel is what an operator would see in a production deployment. In v0.1 it's a visual preview; in v0.2 it wires to the `pulsecraft approve` / `reject` / `edit` / `answer` operator CLI commands.

Total elapsed: 69.1 seconds. Zeta BU had highest priority, triggering the HITL route.

### Scenario 04 — an MLR-sensitive educational update

**Fixture:** `change_007_mlr_sensitive.json` — an HCP-facing educational module update within the medical information portal, restructuring the safety profile section into a system-organ-class table and updating comparative safety terminology.

**SignalScribe reasoning** — the agent proceeds through all three gates, but notably with lower Gate 3 confidence (0.78) than the other scenarios (0.85–0.92):

![Scenario 04 — SignalScribe decides COMMUNICATE (0.85), RIPE (0.92), and READY (0.78). The lower Gate 3 confidence reflects appropriate uncertainty for regulated content.](design/demo/screenshots/Screenshot_scenario_04-1.png)

Gate 3 dropping to 0.78 is the agent being calibrated. Medical information content has more ambiguity than routine feature changes — the before/after description uses regulated terminology that SignalScribe hedges on slightly. The architecture deliberately does not expect SignalScribe to self-police MLR (that's the pre_deliver hook's job); confidence just reflects reading difficulty.

**Policy layer catches regulated content** — BUAtlas produces a personalized message for Gamma BU, then the pre_deliver hook detects MLR-regulated language in the drafted content and routes to human review:

![Scenario 04 — Gamma BU card with AFFECTED + WORTH_SENDING verdicts, followed by a blue "Routed to human review" banner showing mlr_sensitive trigger with scientific_communication: 'efficacy'.](design/demo/screenshots/Screenshot_scenario_04-2.png)

This is the **agent-vs-code architectural principle** in action. BUAtlas judged the message worth sending (Gate 5: WORTH_SENDING at 0.86). The pre_deliver hook, running after BUAtlas, swept the rendered message for restricted terms. It matched "efficacy" as scientific_communication — a category flagged in `policy.yaml` for medical-legal-regulatory review. The hook blocked automated delivery and routed to AWAITING_HITL.

Agents express preferences based on context. Code enforces invariants based on policy. When they diverge, policy wins, and both judgments are logged for calibration.

**Operator review panel** — the terminal state presentation:

![Scenario 04 — "Awaiting human review" section showing the operator review panel with the four action buttons. A caption notes that in production, operators receive a notification and approve via the pulsecraft approve CLI or a dashboard.](design/demo/screenshots/Screenshot_scenario_04-3.png)

The same operator panel pattern as scenario 03 — but the routing reason differs (mlr_sensitive vs. priority_p0). Different triggers, same governance workflow. An operator reviewing this item can approve, reject, edit, or route the question back to subject matter experts. Total elapsed: 76.7 seconds.

6. **Terminal state** — the outcome with an operator review panel when routed to HITL

Every element is produced by real pipeline execution. No content is hand-crafted for display.
